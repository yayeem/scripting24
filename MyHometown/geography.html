Dumaguete City, Geography


Dumaguete City occupies 3,362 hectares on the southeastern plains of Negros Island in the Philippines. It is situated close to the Banica River's mouth. Dumaguete is the capital city of Negros Oriental. It is known for its flat terrain, stretching about two to six kilometers, which gently rises as it approaches the nearby municipality of Valencia. The city is surrounded by natural beauty including the coastline along the Bohol Sea to the south and the mountains to the north, offering a mix of urban and rural landscapes. Its coastal location and proximity to the sea give Dumaguete strategic importance, making it a vital center for trade, transportation, and education in the region.
