What awaits you in Dumaguete

In Dumaguete, you can explore historical landmarks such as Rizal Boulevard, a great spot for families to relax and take a walk along the coast. Additionally, the area is surrounded by many hotels, restaurants, coffee shops, and bars. If you enjoy visiting churches, you can stop by the Saint Catherine of Alexandria Cathedral, the oldest church on Negros Island and the seat of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Dumaguete. It is also a popular wedding venue for many couples. Next to the church is the 1879 Campanario, a heritage site that was used during the Spanish-Moro conflict in the 1800s to warn against invaders. Visitors also light candles and offer prayers in the area. There is also Quezon Park, a place where my family and I used to hang out every Sunday. It is named after Manuel L. Quezon, the first president of the Philippines. Close to the park is the Dumaguete Presidencia, now a branch of the National Museum. It used to serve as the city hall and was once my elementary school. Every September, we celebrate the Sandurot Festival to honor Dumaguete's rich history. Schools from across the city take part in the festivities, and we enjoy watching the parade, even in the heat of the sun. The vibrant costumes and creative dance performances bring a lot of excitement, making it a fun experience for everyone. Another yearly celebration in the city is the Buglasan Festival, also known as the "Festival of All Festivals." It brings together provinces from all over Negros Oriental. Most of the activities take place around the capitol area, where you can explore a two-week display of the best booths from each province, along with various local products. The area becomes even more vibrant at night. These festivals are among the events I have most enjoyed with my family and friends.

Places to Visit


Rizal Boulevard


RIzal Boulevard is named after Dr. Jose Rizal, the Philippines' national hero, who fought against Spanish colonial rule through peaceful means. His life and execution inspired many Filipinos to push for independence, making this an ideal spot for those interested in the country's history.

Saint Catherine of Alexandria Cathedral


Built between 1754 and 1776, St. Catherine of Alexandria Cathedral is the oldest stone church in Negros and is also known as the Dumaguete Cathedral. Located next to the Belfry Tower, it's a popular spot for both locals and tourists, with many believing the church's patroness grants miracles to the faithful.

National Museum of the Philippines - Dumaguete


The National Museum of the Philippines - Dumaguete is housed in the Old Presidencia Building, which served as the "Seat of Power" for all elected officials in Dumaguete since 1937.

Campanarion de Dumaguete


In the past, pirates frequently raided Dumaguete, stealing wealth and capturing people to sell as slaves. Built in the early 1800s, the Belfry served as a watchtower to warn residents of attacks, and while pirates are no longer a threat, the landmark remains a symbol of the city's resilience and growth.

Quezon Park


Quezon Park is located near the Dumaguete Cathedral and Belfry. After Sunday Mass, families often stroll around the plaza and enjoy the playground. It's also just a short walk from Rizal Boulevard, making it a pleasant spot for walk-lovers to explore the city's landmarks.
